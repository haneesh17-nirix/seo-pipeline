# Habun Restaurant — SEO & GEO Strategy

**Brand:** Habun | **Locations:** Ras Al Khaimah & Sharjah, UAE  
**Approach:** White-hat, authority-building, AI-answer optimised (GEO/AEO)

---

## 1. Why This Strategy Won't Get Flagged

Google's spam policies target three patterns: thin content, unnatural links, and manipulative signals.
Every tactic below is the opposite:

| Risk | How we avoid it |
|------|----------------|
| Keyword stuffing | Keywords placed only where natural; density <2% |
| Thin content | Every page >600 words, original, location-specific |
| Unnatural links | Only earned links (citations, press, directories) |
| Duplicate pages | Each location page is completely unique |
| Spam signals | No PBNs, no link farms, no paid links |
| Cloaking | Identical content served to users and Googlebot |

## 2. Foundation — Get These Right First (Week 1–2)

### 2a. Google Business Profile (GBP) — highest ROI free action
- Create separate GBP listings for RAK and Sharjah
- Categories: `Restaurant` (primary), `Casual dining restaurant`, `Halal restaurant`
- Add every field: hours, menu link, photos (20+ real food photos), attributes
- Post weekly (offers, events, new dishes) — GBP posts are indexed
- Respond to every review within 24 hours — signals trust to Google

### 2b. NAP Consistency (Name, Address, Phone)
Every citation must use **identical** NAP:
```
Habun Restaurant
[Full street address, RAK]
+971-XX-XXX-XXXX
habun.ae
```
Inconsistencies are a local ranking penalty. Audit and fix all listings.

### 2c. Schema Markup — auto-generated by our pipeline
Each page gets JSON-LD for:
- `Restaurant` with `servesCuisine`, `priceRange`, `openingHours`, `geo`
- `LocalBusiness` with `address`, `telephone`, `url`
- `Menu` linked to each dish category
- `BreadcrumbList` for navigation structure
- `FAQPage` on the FAQ section

## 3. Content Strategy — No Spam, High Value

### Three content tiers

**Tier 1 — Location pages (permanent, high priority)**
- `/ras-al-khaimah` — 1,000+ words on the RAK dining scene, Habun's place in it, neighbourhood context, parking, nearby landmarks. Not a brochure — a genuinely useful local guide.
- `/sharjah` — same depth for Sharjah

**Tier 2 — Topic cluster blog (weekly)**
Grouped around 4 pillar topics:

| Pillar | Example posts |
|--------|--------------|
| Dining in RAK | "Best family restaurants in Ras Al Khaimah 2025", "Where to eat near Al Hamra" |
| Dining in Sharjah | "Top casual dining in Sharjah", "Halal restaurants near Sharjah City Centre" |
| UAE food culture | "Eid dining traditions in UAE", "Ramadan iftar spots in Emirates" |
| Habun brand | "Behind the kitchen at Habun", "Our sourcing story", "New menu season" |

**Tier 3 — FAQ & answer content (for AI/voice)**  
Short, direct, factual answers to questions Google and AI assistants surface. See Section 5.

### Language
- All content in **English AND Arabic** — UAE search is bilingual
- Arabic pages target queries like "مطعم رأس الخيمة", "أفضل مطعم الشارقة"
- Use `hreflang` tags to signal language variants to Google

## 5. GEO / AEO — Optimising for AI Answers

**GEO = Generative Engine Optimisation.** When someone asks ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, or Perplexity "best restaurant in RAK", the AI answers by reading web pages. If your page directly answers the question in a structured, citable way, it gets quoted.

### How to structure content for AI citation
Every blog post and location page must contain:
1. A **direct answer sentence** in the first paragraph ("Habun is a casual dining restaurant in Ras Al Khaimah serving...")
2. **Factual, verifiable claims** (address, hours, price range, cuisine type)
3. **FAQ blocks** with question exactly matching natural language ("Is Habun halal?", "Does Habun take reservations?")
4. **Structured data** (schema.org) reinforcing the same facts

### Target AI-answer queries
```
"best restaurant in Ras Al Khaimah"           → /ras-al-khaimah
"family friendly halal restaurant in Sharjah" → /sharjah
"where to eat in RAK"                         → blog: RAK dining guide
"is [restaurant] halal"                       → FAQ on each location page
"restaurants open for lunch in Sharjah"       → GBP + schema openingHours
```

### E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)
Google's quality raters look for these. Build them:
- **Experience**: real food photography, team bios, chef story
- **Expertise**: menu descriptions written with culinary knowledge
- **Authoritativeness**: citations in Time Out, Zomato editorial
- **Trust**: displayed address, phone, VAT number, hygiene certificate

## 7. Technical SEO Checklist

- [ ] Core Web Vitals: LCP <2.5s, FID <100ms, CLS <0.1
- [ ] Mobile-first: >60% UAE searches are mobile
- [ ] HTTPS on all pages
- [ ] Canonical tags on all pages (prevent duplicate content)
- [ ] `hreflang` en/ar on all bilingual pages
- [ ] XML sitemap submitted to GSC (auto-generated by our pipeline)
- [ ] `robots.txt` — allow Googlebot, block `/admin`, `/api`
- [ ] Page speed: compress images (WebP), lazy load, minify JS/CSS
- [ ] Structured data validated in Google Rich Results Test
- [ ] 404 page with useful navigation (not dead ends)
